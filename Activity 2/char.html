THE DEAD POETS
SOCIETY

CHARACTERS

Main Characters:

  1. John Keating (Robin Williams)
    • The new English teacher at Welton Academy.
    • Charismatic, passionate, and unorthodox.
    • Encourages students to think for themselves and seize the day (“Carpe Diem”).
    • Central figure in inspiring change among the boys

  2. Neil Perry (Robert Sean Leonard)
    • Bright, ambitious student with a love for acting.
    • Struggles with his father’s strict control over his future.
    • Revives the Dead Poets Society.
    • His tragic fate is a turning point in the story.

  3. Todd Anderson (Ethan Hawke)
    • A shy, introverted student and Neil’s roommate.
    • Lacks confidence at first but gradually opens up, especially through Keating’s influence.
    • Delivers a powerful emotional moment in the final scene.

Supporting Characters:

  1. Knox Overstreet (Josh Charles)
    • Romantic and idealistic.
    • Inspired by Keating to pursue his feelings for a girl named Chris.
    • Shows growth in confidence and boldness.

  2. Charlie Dalton/Nuwanda (Gale Hansen)
    • Rebellious and outspoken member of the group.
    • Pushes boundaries, sometimes too far.
    • Changes his name to “Nuwanda” as an act of defiance.

  3. Richard Cameron (Dylan Kussman)
    • Conservative and rule-following student
    • Betrays the group to protect himself during the investigation.
    • Represents the cost of conformity.

  4. Steven Meeks (Allelon Ruggiero)
    • Intelligent and geeky student.
    • A loyal member of the Dead Poets Society.
    • Often paired with Pitts.

  5. Gerard Pitts (James Waterston)
    • Quiet and a bit awkward.
    • Good friends with Meeks.
    • Also a dedicated member of the group

  6. Mr. Perry (Kurtwood Smith)
    • Neil’s strict and authoritarian father.
    • Insists Neil follow a pre-planned path to become a doctor.
    • His rigidity has devastating consequences.

  7. Headmaster Nolan (Norman Lloyd)
    • Represents Welton Academy’s traditional, conservative authority.
    • Clashes with Keating’s progressive methods.
    • Leads the investigation into Neil’s death.
